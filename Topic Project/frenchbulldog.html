French Bulldogs

French Bulldogs are a small domestic dog.

Appearance

According to the American Kennel Club, the standards of being labeled a French Bulldog are:

  • Size
    • Frenchies are normally small or medium stature
    • The points should be well distributed and bear good relation
  • Coat
    • Acceptable caot colors are brindle, tan, fawn and white with brindle patches
  • Head
    • The skull should be flat but slightly rounded, with a distinct hollow inbetween the eyes
    • The muzzle should be broad, deep and show the muscles of the cheek
    • The nose is extremely short, with each nostril defined
    • Eyes should be wide-set, and dark
  • Jaws
    • The flews should be black
    • They should hang over the lower jaw and cover the teeth
    • The lower jaw is deep, square, broad and undershot
  • Body
    • The dog is strong and short

Temperament

Like most domestic dogs, French Bulldogs require close contact with humans. They often suffer from separation anxiety and cannot be left alone more than a few hours. If left alone, they can turn to destructive behaviors.
