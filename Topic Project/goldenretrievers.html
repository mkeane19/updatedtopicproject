Golden Retrievers

Golden retrievers are large dogs bred as gun dogs to receive shot game such as ducks and birds during hunting parties. Named retriever after their affinity to retrieve undamaged game.

Subtypes of Gold Retrievers

There are British, American, and Canadian Retrievers.

  • British
    • British Retrievers typically have braod skulls
    • Their forequarters are more muscular than others
    • They have light coats
    • Males are normally between 22-24 inches at the withers, while females are between 20-22 inches to the withers
    • The eyes are dark and round, unlike the American slanted or triangular eyes
    • They can have coat shades of gold or cream
  • American
    • American Retrievers are generally lankier and less muscular than others
    • Males stand 23-24 inches at the withers, while females stand 21.5-22.5 at the withers
    • They tend to have darker coats in various shades of gold
    • They have smooth, coordinated gait
  • Canadian
    • Canadian Retrievers have thinner and darker coats
    • The males stand about 23-24 inches at the withers, and females stand between 21.5-22.5 inches

Temperament

Golden Retrievers are kind, friendly and confident. They're patient with children and are trusting and gentle. They love to work and are able to focus on a given task.
