German Shepherds

The German Shepherd can oftenly be associated with police and government work. Originating from Germany, they have been a relatively new breed, their origins traced to 1899. Primarily used as a herding dog, German Shepherds were used for herding sheep on farms. More recently, they've been used for disability assistance, search-and-rescue, police forces and military work.

Appearance

According to the American Kennel Club, the standards of being labeled a German Shepherd are:

  • Size
    • German Shepherds are normally medium to large dogs
    • Their ideal proportions are 10 to 8.5, longer than tall
  • Coat
    • They typically have a two-layer coat, close and dense with a thick undercoat
    • The two acceptable variants are medium and long
    • Acceptable shades are tan/black or red/black
  • Head
    • Traditionally German Shepherds have a "dome-shaped" head, with a long, square muzzle
    • Also noted are strong jaws and a black nose
    • Eyes are normally medium sized and brown, with a lively, intelligent appearance
    • The ears are large, and stand erect, open at front and parallel, except while moving
  • Body
    • Standard height at withers is 60-65 cm for males and 55-60 cm for females
    • Their ideal proportions are for length to height: 10:8.5
    • There is no standard weight range
  • Intelligence
    • German Shepherds have been specifically breeded for their intelligence
    • They are able to learn various tasks quickly

Temperament

German Shepherds are generally self-assured dogs, most noted by their will to learn and eagerness to have purpose. They can be over-protective of friends and family. They're highly intelligent and obedient.
