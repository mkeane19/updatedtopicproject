The Dog

Canis lupis familiaris, or dogs, are members of the genus Canis, or canines, that form a part of the group wolf-like canids. They have been domesticated for many years and now coexist as man (and woman)'s best friend, and for good reason. For hundreds of years, domesticated dogs have been used for hunting, protection, and company. All dogs, from the large Mastiff, to small Tea Cup Chihuahua, all share in the benefit of a well-established relationship wtih humans.

Types of Dogs
