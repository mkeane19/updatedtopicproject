Bulldogs

Bulldogs, one of the scent hound breeds, were commonly used for bull-baiting, until in 1835 the practice was banned.

Appearance

According to the American Kennel Club, the standards of being labeled a Bulldog are:

  • Size
    • They often have wide shoulders
    • They are normally around 50-55 pounds for males, 40-50 for female
  • Coat
    • The coat is flat and short
    • The coat colors come in red, fawn, brinde, and piebald
  • Head
    • Bulldogs have characteristically wide heads
    • They have black, round, wide-set eyes
    • They have short muzzles with folds around the nose and hanging skin around the neck
    • They have drooling lips and pointed teeth, with an underbite and upturned jaw
  • Jaws
    • Most notably bulldogs have upturned jaws
    • They famously have pronounced mandibular prognathism
  • Tail
    • Their tails are naturally short and either straight or screwed

Temperament

Bulldogs are equable and kind, resolute and courageous according to the American Kennel Club. They're not vicious or agressive, and their demeanor is dignified.
