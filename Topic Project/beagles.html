Beagles

Beagles are small hounds, similar to the foxhound. They're scent hounds, primarily used for hunting hare. They're also used as detection dogs. They're intelligent, but single-minded, and popular as pets.

Appearance

According to the American Kennel Club, the standards of being labeled a Beagle are:

  • Size
    • Generally beagles are between 13-16 inches at the withers
    • They generally weigh between 18-35 pounds
  • Coat
    • Beagles can be tricolor or bicolor
    • Their coat colors range from black, white, brown, lemon, and red
  • Head
    • They have smooth, domed skulls
    • They have medium-length, square muzzles with black, gumdrop noses
    • They have long, soft, low-set ears
    • The eyes are large, and hazel or brown
  • Jaws
    • Their jaws are strong
    • The teeth align over eachother
  • Body
    • Beagles have broad chests that lead into a tapered abdomen
    • They also possess whit tipped, curved tails

Temperament

Beagles possess even tempers and gentle dispositions. They are amiable and neither agressive nor timid. They enjoy company and are easily won over by strangers. They're also singe-minded and very determined.
