Poodles

The poodle is a group of formal breeds that includes the Standard Poodle, Miniature Poodle, and Toy Poodle. They're very common in dog shows, such as the Westminster Kennel Club Dog Show.

Types of Poodles

  • Standard Poodle
  • Miniature Poodle
  • Toy Poodle

Appearance

According to the American Kennel Club, the standards of being labeled a Poodle are:

  • Size
    • Size varies greatly from country to country and breed to breed
  • Coat
    • The coat has a naturally curly texture
    • The coat should be dense
    • They only have a single coat
    • They come in black, white, brown, parti, silver, gray, beige, apricot, red, cream, sable and a variety of colors.
  • Head
    • The eyes should be dark, oval-shaped and alert
    • The ears fold close to the head, at or below eye-level
  • Body
    • They're squarely build and well-proportioned

Temperament

Poodles are highly intelligen, energetic and sociable. They enjoy both intellectual and physical activity. They're highly trainable and excel in obedience.
